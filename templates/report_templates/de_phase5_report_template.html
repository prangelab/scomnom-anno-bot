Phase 5 DE Report

Cluster-level contrast memo template

Contrast Definition

State the cluster, annotation layer, contrast key, and A-versus-B pair. Explain the biological meaning of the contrast and whether it is a simple factor, a within-group comparison, or an interaction.

Verdict

Short one-sentence DE verdict

Give one concise paragraph summarizing what the contrast appears to be doing in this cluster. State whether the conclusion is pseudobulk-backed, cell-level-only, or discordant across sources.

Evidence Summary

Combine testability, DA context, gene-level DE, directionality, and source agreement into one tight evidence section. Say which side is higher, what kind of biological change that side seems to represent, whether DA supports a compositional shift, and whether the pseudobulk and cell-level evidence agree. For sex-related contrasts, include the autosomal readout here as a compact second pass beyond the X or Y-linked hits.

Direction Main Genes Biological Reading
Use real biological sides GENE1, GENE2, GENE3 Short functional summary of the higher side.
Opposite side GENE4, GENE5, GENE6 Short functional summary of the counterprogram if meaningful.

Pathway, Activity, And Enrichment Evidence

Explain how DoRothEA, MSigDB, PROGENy, and any relevant enrichment outputs support or complicate the gene-level story. Name the actual pathways, regulators, or condition-specific enrichment patterns that matter instead of using generic terms.

Validation Plots

Show only the plots that actually clarify the memo. Reuse existing volcanoes, dotplots, violins, heatmaps, and enrichment panels first. If extra plots were needed, say what ambiguity they resolved.

Interpretation

Give one short integrative paragraph explaining what this cluster appears to be doing biologically in the requested contrast and what kind of change it represents.

Caveats Or Next-Step Notes

Use this section only when the signal is weak, technically suspect, inflation-prone, or would benefit from targeted follow-up.
