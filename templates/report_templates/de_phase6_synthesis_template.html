Phase 6 DE Synthesis

Cross-layer contrast memo template

Scope

State the active context, the selected contrast, and the annotation layers included in the synthesis. Explain the biological meaning of the contrast and only name the evidence streams that actually matter for the synthesis.

Cross-Layer Signal

Combine the available-layer summary with the short atlas-level story. Say which layers were synthesized, whether pseudobulk support exists anywhere, which side is more coherent if the contrast is directional, and what kind of change dominates across layers.

Driver Populations And Processes

Combine the broad compartment themes, finer driver populations, and process evidence into one tight section. Explain which fine populations drive the broad signals, which broad effects disappear or sharpen after refinement, and which pathways, regulators, custom panels, or enrichment patterns make that story concrete.

Integrated Interpretation

Turn the broad-to-fine signal into one coherent biological interpretation for the contrast. Translate direction into biology and say what kind of change the contrast represents.

Confidence And Caveats

State whether the synthesis is pseudobulk-backed, cell-level-only, concordant across layers, or still provisional. Be explicit about inflation risk, sparse subclusters, mixed programs, or technical ambiguity when relevant.

Practical Takeaway

End with a short paragraph stating the final biological message for the contrast and which populations matter most.
